# Melodies of Malice: Understanding How AI Fuels the Creation and Spread of Extremist Music
**Source:** GNET Research  
**URL:** https://gnet-research.org/2024/12/11/melodies-of-malice-understanding-how-ai-fuels-the-creation-and-spread-of-extremist-music/  
**Date:** 11 December 2024  
**Author:** Heron Lopes (Leiden University / ICCT)

## Summary
Research on extremism has underscored the role of music in reinforcing far-right ideologies and as a gateway to radicalisation. Generative AI music platforms (Udio, Suno AI) enable online extremists to create and amplify hateful music with unprecedented ease. The Guardian reported Suno AI-generated songs in Facebook groups may have contributed to far-right violence in the UK. This Insight examines how far-right communities on 4chan’s /pol/ board use AI to generate and spread hateful music: weaponising and influencing listeners, sharing prompts and jailbreaking tactics (phonetic tricks, coded language), and mainstreaming content to YouTube, TikTok, and Spotify. Risks include echo chambers of extremist AI music, strategic mainstreaming, and algorithmic boosting. Recommendations: identity verification on AI platforms, cross-platform collaboration, enhanced automated detection and red teams for jailbreaking.

## Key points
- Thousands of posts on /pol/ dedicated to AI music propaganda; "weaponize and influence listeners"
- Suno AI, ChatGPT, Bing AI used for lyrics; phonetic jailbreaking ("nih-gur", "gae") and coded terms ("zogbots", "octopus") evade ToS
- Content in multiple languages (50+ on Suno); genres from rock to classical to reach wider audiences
- Users report "only listening to extremist AI-generated music"; some songs explicitly call for attacks in "hotel lobby, packed cafe, grocery shop"
- Strategic mainstreaming to YouTube/TikTok/Spotify; algorithmic engagement to "get in front of 50,000+ people"
