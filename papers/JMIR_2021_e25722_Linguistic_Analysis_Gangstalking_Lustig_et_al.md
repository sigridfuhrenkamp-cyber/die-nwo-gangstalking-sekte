# Linguistic Analysis of Online Communication About a Novel Persecutory Belief System (Gangstalking): Mixed Methods Study
**Source:** Journal of Medical Internet Research (JMIR) 2021;23(3):e25722  
**URL:** https://www.jmir.org/2021/3/e25722/  
**DOI:** 10.2196/25722  
**Published:** 05 Mar 2021  
**Authors:** Andrew Lustig1,2; Gavin Brookes3; Daniel Hunt4  
1 Dept of Psychiatry, University of Toronto / CAMH; 2 Lancaster University; 3 University of Nottingham

## Abstract
Background: Gangstalking is a novel persecutory belief system whereby those affected believe they are being followed, stalked, and harassed by a large number of people, often numbering in the thousands. The harassment is experienced as an accretion of innumerable individually benign acts such as people clearing their throat, muttering under their breath, or giving dirty looks. Individuals affected congregate in online fora as "targeted individuals" (TIs).

Objective: Characterize the linguistic and rhetorical practices used in a gangstalking forum to construct, develop, and contest the gangstalking belief system.

Methods: Mixed methods: corpus linguistics (225,000-word corpus from gangstalking support forum), keyword analysis, collocation analysis, concordancing, discourse analysis.

Results: The forum served as a site of discursive contest between (1) gangstalking as a widespread, centrally coordinated system of persecution (dominant in corpus) and (2) medicalized discourse framing it as mental disorder. Contributors used presupposition, nominalization, and specialized jargon to construct gangstalking as real and external. They generally rejected mental disorder framing but sometimes labeled others in the forum as mentally ill if accounts were deemed too extreme. Concern with accumulating evidence to prove their position to incredulous others.

Conclusions: Contributors co-constructed an internally coherent persecutory belief system, advanced that gangstalking is real, contested medicalization, supported one another, and commiserated over proving its existence.
