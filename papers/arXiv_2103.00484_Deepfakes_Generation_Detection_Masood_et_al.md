## Abstract
Easy access to audio-visual content on social media, combined with the availability of modern tools (Tensorflow, Keras, open-source trained models, economical computing) and the rapid evolution of deep-learning methods, especially GANs, have made it possible to generate deepfakes to disseminate **disinformation, revenge porn, financial frauds, hoaxes, and to disrupt government functioning**. This paper provides a comprehensive review of existing tools and ML-based approaches for **deepfake generation and detection** for both **audio and visual** deepfakes. For each category: manipulation approaches, current public datasets, key standards for performance evaluation and results. Also discusses open challenges and future directions. Deepfakes are documented as weaponizable for **disinformation, harassment, extortion, and reputation damage** (cited in ChatGPT summary on cybermobbing/deepfakes).
